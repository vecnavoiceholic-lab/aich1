3. Outbreeding Devices NCERT IMPORTANT
Plants prevent self-pollination to promote genetic diversity.
Mechanisms
  1. Dichogamy — male & female mature at different times
    • Protandry — anther matures first (e.g., sunflower)
    • Protogyny — stigma matures first
  2. Herkogamy — physical barrier between anther and stigma
  3. Self-incompatibility — biochemical rejection of self pollen
  4. Unisexuality:
    • Same plant → Monoecious
    • Different plants → Dioecious
  5. Anther and stigma at different positions

5. Pollen–Pistil Interaction NCERT
Recognition + acceptance/rejection of pollen by pistil = Pollen–Pistil Interaction
Steps
  1. Stigma recognizes compatible pollen chemically → hydrates it → stimulates germination
  2. Compatibility reaction — pistil tests pollen via chemical interaction between pollen proteins and stigma/style tissues
    • Compatible pollen → allowed to germinate
    • Incompatible pollen → inhibited/rejected (maintains species integrity)
  3. Pollen tube growth — grows through style intercellularly; directed by chemotropic signals from ovule, synergids, female tissues
  4. Vegetative (tube) cell → forms pollen tube; generative cell → moves into tube → divides → 2 male gametes

Double Fertilization
Syngamy, triple fusion — unique to angiosperms
Key Fact
Discovered by: Nawaschin and Guignard
Occurs in: ALL angiosperms
Inside Embryo Sac (Before Fertilization)
  • 1 egg cell
  • 2 synergids
  • 2 polar nuclei (in central cell)
  • 3 antipodal cells
Two Fusions IMP
1. Syngamy DEFINITION
Fusion of male gamete with egg cell. Male gamete (n) + Egg (n) = Zygote (2n) → Embryo
2. Triple Fusion DEFINITION
Fusion involving three haploid nuclei — male gamete + 2 polar nuclei. Male gamete (n) + 2 Polar nuclei (n+n) = PEN (3n) → Endosperm
⭐ DOUBLE FERTILIZATION = Syngamy + Triple Fusion

Dormancy
Seed does NOT germinate even under favourable conditions. Reasons:
  • Hard seed coat
  • Chemical inhibitors
  • Immature embryo
Importance: Allows seeds to survive harsh/unfavourable periods.

Seed absorbs water via micropyle → embryo resumes growth → seedling emerges

Special Phenomena
Parthenocarpy · Apomixis · Polyembryony
1. Parthenocarpy IMP
Definition: Fruit formation without fertilization → produces seedless fruits
  • Example: Banana
  • Induced by growth regulators
2. Apomixis NCERT
Definition: Formation of seeds without fertilization
  • Common in some grasses
  • Asexual embryo develops from diploid nucellar cells
  • No meiosis + No fertilization
  • Agricultural importance: maintains hybrid characters generation after generation
3. Polyembryony IMP
Definition: Formation of more than one embryo in a seed.
Details
  • Example: Citrus
  • Causes:
    • More than one egg fertilized
    • Embryo from nucellar cells

📌 Summary
Sexual reproduction in flowering plants involves formation of male gametophyte (pollen) in microsporangium and female gametophyte (embryo sac) in megasporangium via meiosis. Pollination (by wind, water, or animals) transfers pollen to stigma. Pollen–pistil interaction determines compatibility. Double fertilization (syngamy + triple fusion) is unique to angiosperms, producing zygote (2n) and PEN (3n). Post-fertilization events include endosperm and embryo development, seed formation, and fruit formation. Special phenomena include apomixis, polyembryony, and parthenocarpy.
